# In-process evaluation of culture errors using morphology-based image analysis

## Citation (NLM)
Imai Y, Yoshida K, Matsumoto M, Okada M, Kanie K, Shimizu K, Honda H, Kato R. In-process evaluation of culture errors using morphology-based image analysis. Regen Ther. 2018;9:15-23. doi: 10.1016/j.reth.2018.06.001

**DOI:** [https://doi.org/10.1016/j.reth.2018.06.001](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.reth.2018.06.001)

---

## Background
- Quality control (QC) is a major bottleneck in industrial-scale manufacturing of cell therapy products
- Lack of effective in-process measurement technology forces reliance on manual microscopic observation for time-consuming and complex cell culture quality assessment
- Patient-derived MSCs exhibit large lot-to-lot variation, potentially triggering unexpected quality changes during manufacturing
- Morphological information has potential as a non-invasive, quantitative in-process measurement tool

## Key Experiment Methods
1. **Intentional error model**: 21 lots of human MSCs (bone marrow-derived, adipose-derived) cultured under standard conditions + 4 types of intentional errors (handling failures, machinery malfunctions)
2. **Time-course morphological profiling**: Quantitative measurement of cell morphological features from microscopic images
3. **Modified PCA visualization**: Real-time identification of lot-to-lot and culture condition differences via morphological transition line plots
4. **Prediction modeling**: Linear regression analysis for cell growth rate and error condition discrimination (>80% accuracy with only 2 days of culture data)
5. **MT method**: Machine learning approach suitable for manufacturing processes with abundant standard data but limited failure data

## Results
- Modified PCA visualization enabled real-time identification of differences between cell lots and culture conditions
- Cell growth rate prediction and error condition discrimination achieved >80% accuracy
- MT method enabled anomaly detection in manufacturing environments with limited failure data
- Morphological information demonstrated as an effective in-process measurement tool for cell manufacturing

## Perspective
- Non-invasive morphology-based image analysis is a key tool for cell therapy manufacturing quality control
- Real-time in-process monitoring enables early detection and response to culture errors
- MT method is well-suited for real manufacturing environments with limited failure data
- Future integration with automated imaging for application across cell manufacturing pipelines
- In-process measurement data can contribute to product quality assurance from a regulatory science perspective
