# Automated microfluidic cell culture of stem cell derived dopaminergic neurons

## Citation (NLM)
Kane KI, Moreno EL, Hachi S, Walter M, Jarazo J, Oliveira MAP, Hankemeier T, Vulto P, Schwamborn JC, Thoma M, Fleming RMT. Automated microfluidic cell culture of stem cell derived dopaminergic neurons. Sci Rep. 2019;9:1796. doi: 10.1038/s41598-018-34828-3

**DOI:** [https://doi.org/10.1038/s41598-018-34828-3](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41598-018-34828-3)

## Background
- Parkinson's disease is characterized by dysfunction and death of midbrain dopaminergic neurons; developing patient-derived in vitro disease models is a major challenge
- Automated platform for long-term maintenance and monitoring of cells in 3D microfluidic cell culture devices is needed
- Manual culture requires essential liquid handling (media dispensing/aspiration); microfluidic culture requires low-volume handling
- SiLA (Standardisation in Laboratory Automation) standard protocol integrates heterogeneous manufacturer devices
- Integration of automated Organ-on-a-Chip culture with time-lapse imaging and electrophysiology monitoring is needed

## Key Experiment Methods
1. **Automated cell culture platform**: Gantry robot (Cartesian coordinate movement) + liquid handler (contact/non-contact dispensing) + incubator + microscope integrated, SiLA standard protocol for communication integration
2. **Microfluidic Organ-on-a-Chip**: 3D microfluidic device culture of Parkinson's disease patient-derived neuroepithelial stem cells, differentiated into midbrain-specific dopaminergic neurons
3. **Time-lapse imaging quality control**: Long-term automated imaging for cell state monitoring, tracking differentiation process
4. **Electrophysiology monitoring**: Calcium imaging confirms electrophysiological activity of differentiated neurons, immunostaining validates differentiation efficiency
5. **Flexible protocol adaptation**: Adaptable to various experimental protocols, quantitative control of key experimental parameters such as perfusion rate
6. **Optimization parameters**: dispensing speed/nanoliter volume, perfusion rate, cell culture conditions, fluid temperature, imaging interval, SiLA device class settings

## Results
- First implementation of automated Organ-on-a-Chip culture platform, successful long-term culture of Parkinson's disease patient-derived cells
- Time-lapse imaging enabled continuous monitoring of dopaminergic neuron differentiation process
- Calcium imaging confirmed electrophysiological activity of differentiated neurons
- Immunostaining validated differentiation protocol efficiency
- SiLA standard protocol successfully integrated heterogeneous manufacturer devices into a single autonomous plant
- Low-volume dispensing/aspiration accuracy achieved in microfluidic culture

## Perspective
- Automated Organ-on-a-Chip contributes to patient-specific disease modeling
- SiLA standardization removes barriers to laboratory automation device integration
- Long-term automated culture is effective for studying neurodegenerative disease progression
- Future expansion to multi-Organ-on-a-Chip parallel processing and high-throughput drug screening
- Integration of automated imaging and electrophysiology monitoring develops as a standard for in vitro disease model validation
