# Automated in vivo enzyme engineering accelerates biocatalyst optimization

## Citation (NLM)
Orsi E, Schada von Borzyskowski L, Noack S, Nikel PI, Lindner SN. Automated in vivo enzyme engineering accelerates biocatalyst optimization. Nat Commun. 2024;15:3447. doi: 10.1038/s41467-024-46574-4

**DOI:** [https://doi.org/10.1038/s41467-024-46574-4](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41467-024-46574-4)

## Background
- Cost-competitive bio-based processes require development of stable and selective biocatalysts
- In vitro enzyme characterization and engineering is mostly low throughput and labor-intensive
- In vivo screening and evolution campaigns are gaining momentum for increasing throughput while diminishing manual labor
- Machine learning supports enzyme engineering efforts by widening the explorable design space
- Integration of ML-guided automated workflows (library generation, hypermutation systems, adaptive laboratory evolution, growth-coupled selection) is needed to accelerate pipelines towards superior biocatalysts

## Key Experiment Methods
1. **ML-guided enzyme design**: Protein structure-function prediction ML models for target enzyme variant prediction, design space exploration
2. **Automated library generation**: Biofoundry-based high-throughput DNA assembly and transformation, automated variant library construction
3. **Hypermutation systems**: In vivo continuous evolution with increased mutation rates, localized hypermutation targeting specific genes
4. **Adaptive laboratory evolution (ALE)**: Growth-coupled selection linking enzyme activity to microbial fitness, continuous evolution in automated culture systems
5. **In vivo growth-coupled selection**: Auxotroph selection strain design linking enzyme activity directly to cell growth, automatic selection of active variants
6. **Optimization parameters**: mutation rate, selection pressure (growth-limiting conditions), library size, evolution rounds, ML model hyperparameters

## Results
- ML-guided automated workflows dramatically improved throughput and speed compared to in vitro enzyme engineering
- In vivo continuous evolution efficiently selected enzyme activity variants through growth-coupled selection
- Hypermutation systems induced localized hypermutation in target genes, minimizing whole-genome mutations
- Biofoundry automated platforms integrated library generation, screening, and selection pipelines
- ML models continuously updated with experimental data, improving next-round design (closed-loop optimization)
- Accelerated development of competitive biocatalysts for circular bio-based economy transition

## Perspective
- Integration of ML + in vivo continuous evolution + automated biofoundries transforms enzyme engineering paradigms
- Self-driving lab concepts are applicable to the entire enzyme optimization process
- Growth-coupled selection is established as a core mechanism for in vivo screening
- Future expansion to simultaneous multi-enzyme optimization, entire metabolic pathway optimization, and new-to-nature reaction design
- Automated enzyme engineering platforms have broad applications in industrial biotechnology, pharmaceutical production, and environmental remediation
