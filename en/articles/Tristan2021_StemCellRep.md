# Robotic high-throughput biomanufacturing and functional differentiation of human pluripotent stem cells

## Background
- hiPSC-based disease modeling, drug screening, and cell therapy require scalable cell manufacturing strategies
- Manual cell culture is variable and labor-intensive, posing challenges for high-throughput applications
- Technical and biological variability, lack of standardization, and limited scale-up methods in hiPSC culture
- Comprehensive automation strategy (2D/3D culture compatible, flexible scale-up/down) is needed

## Key Experiment Methods
1. **Robotic platform**: Developed by NIH NCATS Stem Cell Translation Laboratory, automated all essential steps of hiPSC culture and differentiation under chemically defined conditions
2. **High-throughput hiPSC manufacturing**: Standardized production of billions of hiPSCs from up to 90 patient- and disease-specific cell lines in parallel, automated liquid handling for standardization
3. **Automated multi-lineage differentiation**: Generation of functional neurons, cardiomyocytes, and hepatocytes, chemically defined differentiation protocols automated
4. **Comprehensive molecular and cellular characterization**: Single-cell transcriptomics, mass cytometry, metabolism analysis, electrophysiology to benchmark robotic vs manual culture
5. **High-throughput screening validation**: Functional analysis of neurons, cardiomyocytes, and hepatocytes, validated with Zika virus experiments
6. **Optimization parameters**: liquid handling volumes/speeds, media exchange cycles, passaging timing/ratios, differentiation factor concentrations/timing, 2D/3D culture conditions

## Results
- Robotic platform achieved standardized manufacturing of billions of hiPSCs, parallel processing of up to 90 cell lines
- Automated multi-lineage differentiation generated functional neurons (electrophysiological activity), cardiomyocytes (beating), and hepatocytes (metabolic function)
- Single-cell transcriptomics and mass cytometry compared robotic vs manual culture, confirmed quality equivalence
- Metabolism analysis and electrophysiology validated differentiated cell function
- High-throughput screening platform validated with Zika virus experiments
- Robotic culture reduced batch-to-batch variability and improved reproducibility compared to manual

## Perspective
- Comprehensive robotic automation standardizes hiPSC-based disease modeling, drug screening, and cell therapy manufacturing
- Multi-lineage differentiation automation accelerates tissue-specific cell therapy development
- Single-cell transcriptomics and mass cytometry are core tools for automated culture quality control
- Future expansion to GMP-compliant automated platforms for clinical-grade cell manufacturing
- 90 cell line parallel processing capability enables patient cohort-scale screening
