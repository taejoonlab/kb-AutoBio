# Self-driving laboratories: A paradigm shift in nanomedicine development

## Background
- Nanomedicines have achieved clinical success (e.g., mRNA-LNP COVID-19 vaccines), but preclinical development remains complex and time-consuming
- Optimal nanomedicine design in high-dimensional parameter space (composition, size, surface properties) suffers from the curse of dimensionality, making traditional experimental design inefficient
- Self-driving laboratories (SDLs) combine automated experimental equipment with ML-guided experiment planning to identify optimal candidates with minimal human intervention
- SDL applications in nanomedicine are still limited, and the lack of large datasets constrains data-driven approaches

## Key Experiment Methods
1. **NanoMAP proposal**: Nanomedicine Materials Acceleration Platform - integrating high-throughput experimentation + AI (active learning, few-shot learning) + web-based data sharing application
2. **High-throughput experimentation**: Microfluidic nanoparticle synthesis for efficient exploration of composition and process parameter space
3. **ML-guided experiment planning**: Bayesian optimization and active learning leveraging feedback from previous experiments for sequential exploration
4. **Data curation**: Web-based application for large-scale collection and sharing of nanomedicine property and performance data
5. **Few-shot learning**: Enabling generalizable nanoparticle property prediction from limited data

## Results
- NanoMAP concept proposal and architecture design (Perspective article)
- Review of successful SDL applications in chemistry and materials science: catalysts, photovoltaics, thin films, etc.
- Key requirements for SDL adoption in nanomedicine: drug delivery-AI interdisciplinary collaboration, data standardization, high-throughput characterization
- LNP-mRNA vaccine development case highlights clinical importance of nanomedicines and SDL application potential

## Perspective
- SDL platforms like NanoMAP can dramatically accelerate nanomedicine preclinical development timelines
- Large-scale data curation initiatives must precede AI-driven design approaches
- Broad participation from the pharmaceutical science community and data sharing culture are essential
- Improved bench-to-bedside translation can deliver innovative medicines to patients with life-threatening diseases
- Nanomedicine SDLs can expand beyond composition optimization to stability, toxicity, and biodistribution prediction
