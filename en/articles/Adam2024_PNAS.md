# The automated lab of tomorrow

## Background
- Industry and clinical research have embraced automation, but academic labs still rely heavily on low-cost labor from graduate students and postdocs
- Labs sit idle at night and on weekends, while staff turnover leads to loss of valuable knowledge and experience
- Self-driving labs combining AI and automation are used across biology, chemistry, and materials science for designing and executing repetitive steps, analyzing data, and tuning subsequent experiments
- The Nobel Turing Challenge envisions autonomous laboratories making Nobel-worthy discoveries within 30 years

## Key Experiment Methods
1. **Self-driving lab definition**: Integration of automated experimental equipment + AI/ML, analyzing data and determining next experimental conditions in real time (distinct from high-throughput experimentation)
2. **Ada system** (UBC Berlinguette lab): Robotic arm fabricates perovskite solar cell thin-film materials, where thickness, morphology, and defect chemistry are sensitive to process conditions
3. **Cloud labs**: Carnegie Mellon University's academic cloud lab, Emerald Cloud Lab remote experiment ordering service
4. **Automation workflow integration challenges**: Linking commercial automated instruments (GC autosampler, NMR autosampler, etc.) remains a technical barrier

## Results
- Self-driving labs already applied to smart material design and enzyme discovery beyond human capability
- AI requires high-quality, annotated data streams, which robotic automation workflows provide
- Berlinguette's Ada system autonomously optimizes thin-film performance based on process conditions (additive concentration, annealing time/temperature)
- Nobel Turing Challenge: Launched 2020, targeting Nobel-worthy discoveries by autonomous labs within 30 years

## Perspective
- AI and automation integration has potential to dramatically improve basic science efficiency
- Critics warn of hype and AI system failure risks - human supervision is essential
- Automation infrastructure must precede AI/ML utilization in science
- Cloud labs can democratize access to experimental facilities
- Expansion of autonomous labs will transform the paradigm of scientific research itself
