# Optimizing cell therapy by sorting cells with high extracellular vesicle secretion

## Citation (NLM)
Koo D, Cheng X, Udani S, Baghdasarian S, Zhu D, Li J, Hall B, Tsubamoto N, Hu S, Ko J, Cheng K, Di Carlo D. Optimizing cell therapy by sorting cells with high extracellular vesicle secretion. Nat Commun. 2024;15:4870. doi: 10.1038/s41467-024-49123-1

**DOI:** [https://doi.org/10.1038/s41467-024-49123-1](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41467-024-49123-1)

---

## Background
- Absence of methods to enrich cells with high EV secretion is a critical barrier to clinical translation of EV-based therapeutics
- Current cell sorting methods rely on surface markers that are uncorrelated with EV secretion levels or therapeutic potential
- Heterogeneity in EV secretion among cell therapy products is a potential source of inconsistent therapeutic outcomes
- Single-cell level EV secretion analysis and sorting technology development is needed

## Key Experiment Methods
1. **Nanovial technology**: Nanoscale vials capture and sort millions of single cells based on EV secretion, specific tetraspanin molecular markers (CD9, CD63, CD81) for EV detection
2. **Single-cell EV secretion analysis**: Individual cells captured in nanovials, secreted EVs confined within vials for localized concentration increase, fluorescent antibodies detect tetraspanins
3. **FACS-based high-throughput sorting**: Nanovial-cell complexes sorted by fluorescence intensity (EV secretion) via FACS, high-secreting vs low-secreting MSC separation
4. **Transcriptomic analysis**: RNA-seq of sorted MSCs comparing EV biogenesis and vascular regeneration gene expression profiles
5. **Myocardial infarction mouse model**: High-secreting MSC vs low-secreting MSC implantation, comparison of cardiac function (ejection fraction, fractional shortening) and tissue remodeling
6. **Optimization parameters**: nanovial size, antibody concentration, capture time, FACS gate thresholds, cell density, tetraspanin marker combinations

## Results
- Nanovial technology successfully sorted millions of single cells based on EV secretion levels
- High-secreting MSCs showed distinctly differentiated transcriptomic profiles for EV biogenesis and vascular regeneration
- High-secreting MSCs maintained high EV secretion levels after sorting and regrowth
- High-secreting MSC treatment group showed improved cardiac function (increased ejection fraction and fractional shortening) compared to low-secreting in myocardial infarction mouse model
- Tissue remodeling results (reduced fibrosis, increased angiogenesis in high-secreting MSC implantation group)
- First demonstration that EV secretion-based cell sorting improves cell therapy efficacy

## Perspective
- Nanovial-based single-cell secretion analysis contributes to cell therapy product quality control and efficacy optimization
- EV secretion heterogeneity identified as a major factor in cell therapy outcome variation
- Future multi-biomarker simultaneous detection enables multi-dimensional cell function sorting
- High-secreting cell sorting in patient-derived cell therapies improves treatment consistency
- Automated nanovial platforms contribute to cell therapy manufacturing process standardization
