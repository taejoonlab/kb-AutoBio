# Automating Human Induced Pluripotent Stem Cell Culture and Differentiation of iPSC-Derived Retinal Pigment Epithelium for Personalized Drug Testing

Truong V, Viken K, Geng Z, Barkan S, Johnson B, Ebeling MC, Montezuma SR, Ferrington DA, Dutton JR. Automating human induced pluripotent stem cell culture and differentiation of iPSC-derived retinal pigment epithelium for personalized drug testing. ## Background
- hiPSC-derived cells are valuable for personalized disease modeling and drug screening, but technical complexity and lack of standardization in culture/differentiation protocols limit large-scale studies
- Adherent hiPSC culture relies on repetitive liquid handling (media aspiration/dispensing, cell suspension), making it amenable to automation
- Commercial robotic workstations like TECAN Fluent are needed for reproducible, scalable hiPSC culture and differentiation pipelines
- AMD patient-derived iPSC-RPE can be applied to personalized drug screening

## Key Experiment Methods
1. **TECAN Fluent 780 workstation**: 8-tip Air FCA (liquid handling), 96-well MCA, RGA (gripper arm), Liconic incubator (STX44), Hettich centrifuge, Cytation 1 high-content imager integrated
2. **Automated hiPSC culture**: 6-well adherent culture, daily media exchange automation - plate transfer → Cytation 1 imaging (confluence measurement) → tilt carrier (-5°) → FCA with 1000 µL tip for media aspiration/dispensing → incubator return
3. **Automated passaging**: hypertonic citrate solution treatment 6 min (37°C) → wide bore tip for cell detachment (12 aspiration/dispensing pattern) → 800g 3 min centrifugation → pellet resuspension (6 mL incremental addition, wide bore tip 3× mixing)
4. **Automated iPSC-RPE differentiation**: hiPSC to RPE differentiation using fully defined media, automated delivery of differentiation factors (FGF receptor inhibitor, TGF-β/Activin/Nodal inhibitor, Wnt inhibitor)
5. **Automated drug testing**: AMD patient-derived iPSC-RPE exposed to mitoactive compounds with automated mitochondrial function measurement
6. **Optimization parameters**: liquid handling volumes, aspiration/dispensing speeds, tip types (standard/wide bore), tilt angles, incubation times, centrifugation conditions, confluence-based passaging timing

## Results
- Successful culture and differentiation of multiple hiPSC lines on TECAN Fluent, improved reproducibility and scalability compared to manual culture
- Automated differentiation of AMD patient-derived iPSC-RPE lines, utilized for downstream drug testing
- Automated drug testing workflow measured mitochondrial function after mitoactive compound exposure
- Proof-of-principle on commercial platform enables broad dissemination of methodology
- End-to-end automation of hiPSC culture and differentiation demonstrated effective for personalized drug screening

## Perspective
- Commercial robotic workstations improve accessibility of hiPSC-based research and therapeutic development
- Integration with automated imaging enables long-term culture assessment and data acquisition
- Patient cohort-scale iPSC-RPE production enables personalized drug screening
- GLP/cGMP-compliant automated pipelines can expand to cell therapy manufacturing
- Liquid handling automation is key to improving standardization and reproducibility in hiPSC research
