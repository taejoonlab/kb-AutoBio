# An Automated Organoid Platform with Inter-organoid Homogeneity and Inter-patient Heterogeneity

## Citation (NLM)
Jiang S, Zhao H, Zhang W, Wang J, Liu Y, Cao Y, Zheng H, Hu Z, Wang S, Zhu Y, Wang W, Cui S, Lobie PE, Huang L, Ma S. An automated organoid platform with inter-organoid homogeneity and inter-patient heterogeneity. Cell Rep Med. 2020;1(9):100161. doi: 10.1016/j.xcrm.2020.100161

**DOI:** [https://doi.org/10.1016/j.xcrm.2020.100161](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.xcrm.2020.100161)

---

## Background
- Conventional organoid technologies rely on manual manipulation, resulting in large batch-to-batch and organoid-to-organoid variability, and require 4-6 weeks, limiting timely patient-specific drug screening
- Single-cell-derived organoids struggle to capture heterogeneity, and bulk culture produces size and cellular composition inconsistency
- To capture tumor heterogeneity while shortening model establishment time, 1,000-2,000 cells (statistically representing tumor heterogeneity) must be cultured in defined Matrigel volumes
- High-throughput, automated organoid distribution is needed to reduce labor costs and batch-to-batch variability

## Key Experiment Methods
1. **M1 module (microfluidics-based organoid fabrication)**: Customized droplet-based microfluidic system, Matrigel solution maintained at 4°C in cooler, cell-laden Matrigel droplet generation in PTFE tubing (oil phase HFE7000 + Matrigel via 3-way PDMS connector), sol-gel transition in 37°C water bath
2. **M2 module (3D droplet printing)**: Customized droplet printer with stepwise XY motion control, tubing outlet locomotion patterns individual organoid precursors into 96-well plates, HFE7000 volatility eliminates residue
3. **Optimization parameters**: flow rates (oil 30 µL/min, droplet 20 µL/min), printer speed (XY 500 mm/s, Z 20 mm/s), 2s standing time per well, synchronization of droplet formation frequency with XY motion
4. **Single organoid per well**: >95% success rate (max 4 missing per 96-well), 100-1,000 organoid precursors generated in <10 min, 1 organoid/second patterning
5. **Rapid development**: ~0.08 µL per droplet, ~1,506±13 cells encapsulated (cell density 2.0×10^7 cells/mL), >400 µM large organoids in 5-7 days (vs. conventional 4-6 weeks)

## Results
- Mouse lung/liver/kidney healthy organoids and human tumor organoids all monodisperse in size/distribution, significantly reduced inter-organoid variability
- Healthy organoids recapitulated epithelial self-organization, tumor organoids showed reduced epithelium - reflecting parental tissue characteristics
- Histological analysis: microscale recapitulation of epithelial organization similar to parental tissues, immunofluorescence (E-cadherin, EpCAM) confirmed epithelial monolayer domains
- Gene expression: organoids showed similar gene-expression profiles to parental tissues, tumor organoids recapitulated 97% of gene mutations in parental tumors
- Drug response prediction accuracy >80% across 21 patients, clearly reflecting inter-patient heterogeneity
- High-throughput drug screening achievable in 1 week - shortened timeline from patient tumor sampling to drug evaluation and therapy recommendation

## Perspective
- Automated organoid platform solves the timeliness challenge of personalized cancer therapy screening
- Simultaneous achievement of inter-organoid homogeneity and inter-patient heterogeneity is central to personalized medicine
- Integration of microfluidics and 3D printing transforms organoid fabrication and distribution paradigms
- Future patient-derived organoid biobanks and real-time drug response prediction can realize precision medicine
- Automation reduces labor costs and eliminates batch-to-batch variability, improving clinical applicability
