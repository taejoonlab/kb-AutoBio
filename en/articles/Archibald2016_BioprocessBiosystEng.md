# Comparability of automated human induced pluripotent stem cell culture: a pilot study

## Citation (NLM)
Archibald PRT, Chandra A, Thomas D, Chose O, Massouridès E, Laâbi Y, Williams DJ. Comparability of automated human induced pluripotent stem cell culture: a pilot study. Bioprocess Biosyst Eng. 2016;39:1847-1858. doi: 10.1007/s00449-016-1659-9

**DOI:** [https://doi.org/10.1007/s00449-016-1659-9](https://doi.org/10.1007/s00449-016-1659-9)

## Background
- Consistent and robust manufacturing is essential for cell therapy translation; automation can improve quality control, scalability, reproducibility, and economics
- Comparison of manual centrifugation vs automated non-centrifugation process steps in hiPSC culture is needed
- Transferability of automated hiPSC manufacturing protocols between independent laboratories requires validation
- Impact of cryopreservation and thawing processes on hiPSC culture growth and characteristics needs assessment

## Key Experiment Methods
1. **CompacT SelecT automated cell culture platform** (TAP Biosystems/Sartorius): Automated hiPSC culture, supports non-centrifugation process steps
2. **Manual centrifugation vs automated non-centrifugation comparison**: hiPSC line (VAX001024c07) culture, comparison of cell morphology, diameter, yield, aggregation rate, pluripotency markers, and differentiation markers
3. **Cell characterization**: Microscopic morphology observation, cell diameter measurement, flow cytometry for pluripotency markers (SSEA, TRA) and differentiation marker quantification
4. **Cumulative population doublings (CPDs)**: Cell proliferation rate tracking, analysis of cell yield variation trends after automated process step utilization
5. **Inter-laboratory transfer**: Same automated protocol performed in independent laboratories (Loughborough UK ↔ I-Stem France), results compared
6. **Optimization parameters**: centrifugation presence/absence, ROCK inhibitor treatment, seeding density, media exchange cycle, cryopreservation/thawing protocol

## Results
- Comparable morphology and cell diameter observed in hiPSCs cultured using either manual or automated process steps
- Non-centrifugation hiPSC populations showed greater cell yields, higher aggregation rates, increased pluripotency marker expression, and decreased differentiation marker expression
- Trend for decreased variability in cell yield observed after automated process step utilization
- Cryopreservation and thawing processes demonstrated detrimental effects on hiPSC culture growth and characteristics
- Automated hiPSC manufacturing protocol successfully transferred between independent laboratories

## Perspective
- Automation contributes to improving reproducibility, scalability, and economics of hiPSC manufacturing
- Non-centrifugation processes may be advantageous over centrifugation for cell yield and pluripotency maintenance
- Cryopreservation/thawing protocol optimization is important in automated culture pipelines
- Inter-laboratory protocol transferability contributes to hiPSC manufacturing standardization
- Future expansion to GMP-compliant automated platforms for commercial cell therapy manufacturing
