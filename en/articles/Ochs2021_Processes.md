# Fully Automated Cultivation of Adipose-Derived Stem Cells in the StemCellDiscovery—A Robotic Laboratory for Small-Scale, High-Throughput Cell Production Including Deep Learning-Based Confluence Estimation

## Background
- Cell-based personalized therapies require automation to achieve cost efficiency and broad accessibility
- Patient-derived cell expansion is labor-intensive with high batch-to-batch variability; small individual batch sizes require high throughput
- hMSCs (human mesenchymal stem cells) can differentiate into adipose, cartilage, or bone cells and have tissue regeneration and immune modulation functions
- Planar culture is suitable for small-scale screening, but automation is needed to improve standardization, throughput, and reproducibility

## Key Experiment Methods
1. **StemCellDiscovery system**: Fully automated robotic laboratory developed by Fraunhofer IPT, parallel cultivation of adherent cells, focused on adipose-derived hMSCs
2. **In-line visual quality control**: High-speed microscopy + deep learning-based image processing for automated confluence estimation, hMSC detection and confluence calculation at various densities
3. **Automated cultivation workflow**: seeding → media exchange → confluence monitoring → passaging timing decision → cell expansion, fully automated
4. **Deep learning confluence estimation algorithm**: CNN-based segmentation, trained on various density conditions, validated against manual confluence estimation
5. **Production environment simulation**: Simulation software modeling of StemCellDiscovery capacity, prediction of daily plate throughput under high-throughput conditions
6. **Optimization parameters**: confluence threshold (passaging trigger), media exchange cycle, seeding density, passaging ratio, robotic processing speed

## Results
- StemCellDiscovery successfully achieved fully automated expansion of adipose-derived hMSCs, with confluence estimation algorithm automatically determining passaging timing
- Deep learning confluence estimation algorithm successfully detected hMSCs and calculated confluence at various cell densities
- Simulation results showed the robotic laboratory can process more than 95 cell culture plates per day
- Economic viability for personalized therapy cell production under small-scale, high-throughput conditions demonstrated
- Automated cultivation improved standardization, reproducibility, and throughput compared to manual culture

## Perspective
- Fully automated robotic laboratories are key infrastructure for small-scale, high-throughput cell therapy production
- Deep learning-based confluence estimation can evolve as an automation standard for cell culture quality control
- Convergence of Industry 4.0 and biotech transforms personalized therapy manufacturing paradigms
- Future expansion to multi-cell line simultaneous processing, differentiation protocol automation, and real-time quality monitoring
- Automated production pipelines contribute to cell therapy commercialization and insurance coverage
